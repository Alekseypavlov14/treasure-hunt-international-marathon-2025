Answers
  1. Transilvania University was founded in 1948.
  2. Brașov’s old name is Kronstadt, and the city's symbol is a crown with tree roots.
  3. After a fire in 1689, the church’s walls turned black—giving it the name "Black Church".
  4. The gates are named after St. Catherine’s Monastery, which once stood nearby.
  5. The bastion’s walls are 4 meters thick!
  6. The architectural style is Art Nouveau.
  7. The wolf in front of the building symbolizes the connection between Romania and Rome—the wolf is Rome’s emblem.
  8. The church was built in 1235!
